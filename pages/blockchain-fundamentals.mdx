## Blockchain Architecture
The architecture of a blockchain network is designed to ensure the integrity, security, and decentralization of the system. At the core of this architecture are several key components:

**Distributed Ledger**: A blockchain is a distributed ledger, where transaction data is stored across a network of nodes, rather than a centralized location. This decentralized approach enhances the network's resilience and security.

**Blocks and Transactions**: The transaction data is organized into blocks, and each block is cryptographically linked to the previous block, forming a "chain" of blocks - hence the name "blockchain." This chain-like structure ensures the immutability of the recorded transactions.

<Callout type="info">
The process of adding new blocks to the blockchain is known as "mining," and it is typically done by network participants, known as "miners," who use computational power to validate and secure the transactions.
</Callout>

**Cryptographic Hashing**: Blockchain networks extensively utilize cryptographic hashing algorithms, such as SHA-256 and Keccak-256 (also known as SHA-3), to secure the data within the blocks and ensure the integrity of the blockchain. These hashing algorithms convert data of arbitrary size into a fixed-size, unique output, known as a "hash," which is used to identify and verify the contents of each block.

**Peer-to-Peer (P2P) Network**: Blockchain networks operate on a decentralized, peer-to-peer architecture, where all participating nodes (known as "peers") communicate directly with each other, without the need for a central authority. This distributed network structure enhances the resilience and security of the system, as there is no single point of failure.

**Consensus Mechanisms**: Blockchain networks employ various consensus mechanisms, such as Proof of Work (PoW) and Proof of Stake (PoS), to ensure that all nodes in the network agree on the valid state of the blockchain. These consensus algorithms define the rules and incentives for network participants to validate and add new blocks to the chain.

<Callout type="warning">
It's important to note that the specific implementation and design choices of a blockchain network can vary, and different blockchain platforms may have their own unique architectural features and consensus mechanisms. Understanding the general principles, however, will provide a solid foundation for further exploration of the DeFi ecosystem.
</Callout>

By understanding the fundamental architecture and components of a blockchain network, you'll be better equipped to navigate the complexities of the DeFi landscape and build robust, decentralized applications.
